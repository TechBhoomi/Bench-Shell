Steps to Close Apps - Using Task Manager

  1. Open Task Manager:
    • Press Ctrl + Shift + Esc on your keyboard.
    • Alternatively, right-click on the taskbar at the bottom of the screen and select "Task Manager" from the context menu.
    • Another method is to press Ctrl + Alt + Delete, then select "Task Manager" from the options.
  2. Select and Close the App:
    • Click on the app or process you want to close to select it.
    • After selecting the app, click on the "End Task" or "End Process" button at the bottom-right corner of the Task Manager window.
    • A confirmation prompt may appear; click "End Task" to confirm and close the selected app.
  3. Verify Closure:
    • Check that the app has been closed by looking at the application window or by rechecking the Task Manager to confirm it's no longer listed among the running processes.

Steps to Close Apps - Using PowerShell

  1. Open PowerShell as Administrator:
    • Search for "PowerShell" in the Windows search bar.
    • Right-click on "Windows PowerShell" or "PowerShell" in the search results.
    • Select "Run as administrator" from the context menu.
  2. Execute PowerShell Commands to Close Apps:
    • Enter the following command in the elevated PowerShell window:
      • Get-Process
      Stop-Process -Force
      Get-Process -Name "ProcessName" | Stop-Process -Force
    • Replace "ProcessName" with the name of the app or process you want to close. For example:
      • Get-Process -Name "Notepad" | Stop-Process -Force
    • Execute the command by pressing the Enter key.
    • Repeat the command with different process names to close multiple apps.
  3. Verify App Closure:
    • Check that the apps have been closed by verifying their absence in the Task Manager or by attempting to restart the closed apps.
